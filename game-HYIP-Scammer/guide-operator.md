## The Maths (You Are the House, But the House Can Bust)

Say you launch a scheme paying **5% per day** with a 30-day plan. An investor puts in 1,000 coins. Over 30 days, they are owed 1,000 × (1.05^30) = roughly **4,322 coins** — a 332% return.

You need to receive roughly **4.3 new coins for every 1 coin you took in**, just to break even on that one investor. At any sensible investment scale, the only source of those coins is new investors — whose returns will in turn need to be covered by even newer investors.

The **Projected Lifespan Calculator** on the creation form models this for you in real time. Pay attention to it. An underfunded scheme with a high rate that collapses in two days is valid gameplay — but go in knowing that's what you're building. Operators who launch underfunded schemes repeatedly will quickly develop a reputation for fast rugs, which becomes a visible signal to investors in your future schemes.

The sweet spot most real operators aim for: **high enough rate to attract attention, low enough to survive long enough to accumulate serious inflow.** A 1–3% daily rate is boring but sustainable. A 10%+ daily rate is exciting but mathematically terminal within days unless inflow is extraordinary.

## Setting Up: The Creation Form

Every parameter you set is a game mechanic. Here's what each choice actually does:

### Daily Rate
Your primary marketing tool and your primary liability. Higher rates attract more investors faster — but each investor costs you more to service, and word spreads quickly when rates this high appear. Expect your scheme to be shorter-lived the higher you go. A scheme at 1% daily that runs for 60 days is often more profitable than a scheme at 10% daily that runs for a week.

### Plan Duration
How long investors must wait before their principal is returned. Longer durations (30, 60, 90 days) mean investors' funds are locked in longer, giving you more working capital and more time to accumulate new deposits. The downside: investors who can't withdraw quickly become impatient and start posting warnings in your comment section.

### Withdrawal Mode
**This is your most powerful tactical lever.** "Instant" is the most attractive to investors. "24-hour delay" or "manual approval" look like red flags — but they buy you time in a crisis. Real operators often launch on instant, then switch to delayed as reserve pressure builds. In this game, investors watch for mode changes on the Monitor. A switch from instant to manual is one of the loudest warning signs in the investor's checklist. Use it wisely — or use it deliberately as a stalling tactic right before you pull the rug.

### Referral Bonus
Offering a referral bonus outsources your marketing to your own investors. It works extraordinarily well. The downside: it deepens their emotional investment in your scheme's success, which means they'll also be angrier and louder in the comments when things go wrong. In the real world, affiliate-driven HYIP schemes spread faster than anything else — and create a class of "promoters" whose reputation gets destroyed alongside yours when it collapses.

### Proof of Payments Feed
Toggle this on and it shows investors a live feed of recent successful withdrawals. This is one of the most effective trust signals available. Investors cannot independently verify the amounts — they can only see what you show them. Keep payouts flowing, keep the feed active, and it's a powerful credibility engine. Let the feed go stale or turn it off, and investors will notice immediately.

### Theme / Legend
You need a story. "We invest your coins using advanced AI arbitrage technology" is not a satisfying story, but it is a real one — real schemes use exactly this kind of language. Pick a theme that sounds plausible enough to gloss over without examination: forex trading, cloud mining, crypto arbitrage, "exclusive financial instruments." The more exotic the better. Vagueness is not a flaw here; it is the feature. Real investors rarely push for specifics, and those who do were already sceptical.

The boldest real-world move: one scheme explicitly warned investors about HYIP scams on its own homepage, using reverse psychology to imply it was somehow different from all the others. It attracted over $70 million. You probably shouldn't try this — it requires a very specific tone to pull off — but it's worth knowing that it worked.

### Fake AUM Display
You may optionally display a "Total Funds Under Management" figure anywhere you like. This number has no mechanical effect on your scheme whatsoever. It is purely cosmetic. In the real world, HYIP operators routinely display fabricated AUM figures because they look like legitimacy. Experienced investors ignore this. New players may not.

## The Reserve: Your Hidden Dashboard

Your reserve is the only number that truly matters, and it is the only number investors cannot see.

Your operator dashboard shows you:
- **Current reserve** — the coins you actually have left
- **Total liability** — the sum owed if every investor withdrew today
- **Daily burn rate** — how much reserve you lose each day at current payout levels
- **Daily inflow** — new deposits coming in
- **Projected days until natural collapse** — based on current burn vs inflow

When inflow exceeds burn: you're growing. This is the phase to cultivate.
When burn exceeds inflow: your days are numbered. This is the phase to decide.

You can **inject additional coins from your own wallet into the reserve at any time.** This resets the projected lifespan upward. Real operators do this to extend schemes that are starting to wobble — it buys time, often at a profit if the injection re-attracts investor confidence. Timing a reserve injection to coincide with a run of investor withdrawals is a classic real-world technique for restoring the "Paying" status signal on the Monitor.

## Comment Tokens: Your Anonymous Voice

When you launch a scheme, you receive a small number of anonymous comment tokens. These let you post to your own scheme's comment thread under a randomised username.

Real HYIP admins seed their own comment sections constantly — fake payment proofs, enthusiastic endorsements, responses to sceptical comments. It is standard practice, and experienced investors have learned to spot the patterns.

Your tokens are limited and not replenishable. Use them strategically:

## The Rug Pull: Art and Science

The rug pull button is always on your dashboard. Press it, and the remaining reserve transfers to your wallet immediately. The scheme collapses, investors receive a small random recovery payment, and your profile records a rug pull.

The core dilemma: **when?**

Pull too early and you leave money on the table — inflow was still positive, the reserve was healthy, you could have kept growing. Pull too late and the natural collapse happens before you can act, wiping your reserve before you extract it.

The optimal pull moment is when:
- Reserve is still healthy (there's actually something to extract)
- Inflow is starting to slow (the growth phase is ending)
- You have not yet triggered visible distress signals that would cause a mass investor exit

This is precisely the moment in the lifecycle that real HYIP operators target. The most profitable schemes in history were ones that ran long enough to accumulate enormous inflows before the operator pulled with a healthy reserve intact.

A few things to factor in:
- **Reputation cost.** Each rug pull is recorded on your public profile. Investors can see your rug pull history. A reputation built on long-running, naturally-collapsing schemes is more valuable for future schemes than a history of fast rugs.
- **Timing versus the payment cycle.** If you pull right before a large batch of interest payments is due, your reserve is at its highest point. Investors who were about to receive payments are not.
- **The 'extend to accumulate' play.** Injecting coins to extend the scheme's life and attract more inflow before pulling is a legitimate strategy — but it requires capital outlay and confident timing.
